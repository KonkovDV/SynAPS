# Investor Letter: SynAPS vs APS Infimum at Moskabel

> **Terms and confidence labels: [GLOSSARY](GLOSSARY_2026_04.md).** | [ГЛОССАРИЙ (RU)](GLOSSARY_2026_04_RU.md)

Status: active
Audience: potential investor evaluating SynAPS against a real vertical APS reference case

Dear Investor,

You asked me a hard and important question.

If Moskabel already has APS Infimum, what would SynAPS realistically change? Would replacing APS Infimum with SynAPS create more economic value for the plant?

I've thought about this carefully, and the academically defensible answer has three parts.

First, not yet as a full replacement in 2026. Second, possibly yes as a longer-term kernel and augmentation thesis. Third, only after a shadow-mode validation program proves additional value beyond the current APS Infimum baseline.

That distinction is the core of the investment decision.

## 1. Short answer

APS Infimum is the stronger choice for Moskabel right now — as a live, vertical production-planning product.

SynAPS is the stronger choice as an open, technically auditable, cross-industry planning-kernel investment thesis.

If you're asking: "Which system would I trust to remain the production system of record at Moskabel tomorrow morning?" — the answer is APS Infimum.

If you're asking: "Which asset is easier to diligence, benchmark, extend, and position as a portable category-defining kernel across industries?" — the answer is SynAPS.

## 2. What APS Infimum already proves

APS Infimum has several forms of evidence that carry real commercial weight.

Public MOSITLAB surfaces and lecture-slide materials indicate the following. It's sold as a real product, not merely described as a thesis. It has visible operational workflows — batching, Gantt planning, workstation-level views. It targets concrete cable-production pain: changeovers, work in progress, containers, work-center allocation. It sits inside a broader ecosystem that includes MES, CoMod, RFID, and adjacent manufacturing products. And it's publicly associated with Moskabel-scale operations.

This matters. It means APS Infimum is not a concept memo.

## 3. What SynAPS already proves

SynAPS proves a different kind of strength.

Unlike APS Infimum, I can already be technically inspected in depth by any external diligence team. What you can check right now: open schema drafts, visible Python solver code, local repair logic, a benchmark harness, a fresh 27/27 test pass, a benchmark smoke run demonstrating end-to-end execution, and an investor packet with explicit claim boundaries, red-team review, methods appendix, and evidence register.

That doesn't make SynAPS operationally ahead of APS Infimum at Moskabel. But it does make SynAPS substantially easier to diligence as an investable technical kernel.

## 4. Why a direct replacement thesis is weak in 2026

The mistake would be to assume that because SynAPS is more open and more disciplined, it must therefore create more value at Moskabel right now.

That conclusion isn't supported by the evidence.

Today SynAPS still lacks several things that APS Infimum appears to have in production: a mature plant-facing UI with operational workflows, proven integration into the Moskabel execution stack, validated cable-domain calibration at production-system-of-record level, and externally observed operator adoption inside that plant context.

Put simply: SynAPS may be the better venture asset, but APS Infimum is the lower-risk operating asset for Moskabel in 2026.

## 5. Economic conclusion for Moskabel

### 5.1 Evidence-weighted answer

On an evidence-weighted basis, replacing APS Infimum with SynAPS at Moskabel in 2026 would not be expected to produce positive near-term net savings.

The rigorous conclusion: immediate incremental savings versus staying on APS Infimum are unproven. Near-term replacement economics are likely negative once I factor in integration, validation, retraining, and transition risk. SynAPS becomes economically interesting only if it can prove additional steady-state benefit beyond APS Infimum's current baseline — and that requires a controlled shadow-mode program.

### 5.2 Counterfactual threshold

Using a five-year risk-adjusted replacement model with one stabilization year and a 15–20% discount range, SynAPS would need to deliver the following additional annual benefit above APS Infimum:

| Replacement scenario | Required incremental annual benefit |
| --- | ---: |
| Minimal | `36.3M–41.7M RUB/year` |
| Base | `64.4M–74.2M RUB/year` |
| Full | `100.7M–115.9M RUB/year` |

Those thresholds equal approximately `0.18%–0.58%` of the `~20B RUB/year` production turnover publicly associated with the APS Infimum case.

### 5.3 What that means in plain language

For SynAPS to justify replacing APS Infimum at Moskabel, matching isn't enough. It would have to exceed it by a margin wide enough to cover integration work, parallel validation, operator retraining, temporary execution risk, and the loss of a system already embedded in the cable-production stack.

My current evidence doesn't support that claim.

## 6. How to interpret APS Infimum's public economics

APS Infimum's public numbers are directionally impressive, but they're not fully reconciled — and an honest analysis has to acknowledge that distinction.

The publicly visible figures: 23M RUB for Q1 2024, +14% productivity, +8% net profit, -46% changeover time losses, -12% WIP, -11% average order fulfillment time, a lecture-slide claim of 27 days / roughly 1.2B RUB per year, and a product-page headline near 1.4B RUB per year.

These are useful. But they're not the same thing as an audited investor-quality economic model.

So the right discipline is: treat the 23M Q1 2024 figure as the harder lower-band signal based on a more direct effect measurement; treat the 1.2B–1.4B/year band as a management-headline range rather than audited cash savings; and don't underwrite a replacement case on the assumption that SynAPS can beat those numbers without plant-specific proof.

## 7. My assessment

If the investment question is plant economics at Moskabel in 2026 — don't frame SynAPS as a rip-and-replace substitute for APS Infimum. That would be economically and evidentially too aggressive.

If the investment question is category creation and long-term platform value — SynAPS is genuinely interesting because it offers something APS Infimum doesn't currently expose publicly: open technical inspectability, reproducible benchmarks, stronger claim tracking, and a more portable cross-industry kernel story.

My conclusion, then, is this. APS Infimum validates the market pain and the existence of real economic value in industrial APS. SynAPS is the better candidate for a transparent, extensible, cross-industry kernel investment thesis. And the correct go-to-market path is augmentation first, not immediate replacement.

## 8. What I'd recommend

If you want to test SynAPS against APS Infimum seriously, here's the right program.

Run SynAPS in shadow mode against historical and live planning datasets from Moskabel. Compare schedule quality, plan stability, changeover time, WIP, and fulfillment latency against APS Infimum. Don't cut over until SynAPS proves incremental annual value above the replacement threshold band I showed in section 5. And separate plant-operations savings from strategic platform value — portability, open benchmarking, reduced vendor lock-in.

## 9. The bottom line

For Moskabel in 2026, APS Infimum remains the economically safer operating system. For a long-horizon investor looking for a portable and auditable planning kernel, SynAPS is the more interesting strategic asset.
